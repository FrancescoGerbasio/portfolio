About me

Francesco
Gerbasio.

I'm a UX/UI Designer and Localization QA based in Madrid — obsessed with the gap between how things look and how they feel.

Originally from Italy 🇮🇹, I've been living in Madrid for a few years now — and it's hard to leave when the city is this alive. I started in design because I couldn't stop noticing when things felt off, and I couldn't stop imagining how they could be better.

By day, I catch the details that shouldn't ship — as a Localization QA at Universally Speaking, I make sure games feel native in Italian, not just translated. Before that, I spent time as a freelance UX/UI designer, turning early-stage ideas into polished interfaces.

I believe good design is invisible. It's the button that doesn't make you think, the animation that feels inevitable, the word choice that sounds like it was always meant to be there.

Outside of work, you'll find me building playlists I never finish, exploring a new city, or deep in an open-world RPG I'm definitely going to 100% complete this time.

What I care about

Values
🎯
Precision over perfection

I'd rather ship something that works exactly right than something visually elaborate that confuses people. Every detail should earn its place.

🌍
Global thinking

Being multilingual shaped how I see design — a UI that works in one language might completely break in another. Localisation is a design problem.

🔍
Curiosity as a craft

The best design decisions come from asking "why?" one more time than everyone else. I'm genuinely curious about how people experience things.

⚡
Speed with intention

Moving fast doesn't mean being sloppy. I've learned to iterate quickly while keeping the quality bar high — because momentum and craft aren't opposites.

🎮
Play is serious work

Gaming, music, travel — they're not distractions from design, they're where I learn the most about storytelling, immersion, and emotional design.

🤝
Collaboration over ego

The best work I've done has always come from genuine collaboration. I'd rather a great idea come from anyone on the team than defend a mediocre one that's mine.

01 — Problem

Abruzzo has a lot to offer. None of it is easy to find.

Abruzzo is one of Italy's least digitally represented regions. Events, activities, and local experiences are scattered across municipal websites, Facebook groups, WhatsApp chats, and niche organiser pages. For tourists, that means missed opportunities. For locals, it's the same.

The goal was a single platform connecting tourists, residents, and local businesses — centralising discovery without flattening the character of the region into a generic travel feed.

02 — Research

The gap wasn't in Abruzzo. It was in the infrastructure around it.

Market research confirmed what personal experience already suggested: compared to Tuscany or the Amalfi Coast, Abruzzo has almost no dedicated digital layer for discovery. The two closest competitors — Airbnb Experiences and the local app Abruzzocosafare — both exist but neither covers the full picture.

🗂️
Scattered information
Finding events meant checking multiple websites, social media pages, and organiser platforms separately. No single source of truth existed for what's actually happening.
📅
Missed opportunities
Without a centralised source, both locals and tourists regularly miss events they would have attended. The problem isn't interest — it's visibility.
🏪
Invisible local businesses
Small operators and event organisers had no scalable way to reach an audience. Word of mouth and Facebook posts were the primary distribution channels.
🌍
Three distinct user needs
Tourists need curation and guidance. Residents want to discover what's nearby. Businesses need a channel. One app, three different jobs to support.
03 — Design decisions

One app. Three audiences. One visual identity rooted in the region.

1

Identity

Colours from the Abruzzo flag

The visual identity was grounded directly in Abruzzo's regional flag — not a generic travel palette. Green and white with red accents. The design immediately communicates where it belongs.

2

Structure

Centralised discovery

Events, activities, and local businesses in one feed. Filterable by category, date, and location — so both a tourist planning a weekend and a local checking what's on tonight find what they need.

3

Audience

Wireframes across three user journeys

Low-fidelity wireframes were built separately for each of the three user groups before moving to hi-fi, to validate that the same architecture served different entry points and goals.

4

Growth

Built for iteration

Next steps scoped out: social features for sharing events, in-app ticketing with organiser partnerships, and analytics tools for local businesses to track engagement.
